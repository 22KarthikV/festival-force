THE VOODOO ROOMS - STAFF HANDBOOK
Edinburgh Fringe Festival 2025

BARTENDER ROLE GUIDE
====================

Welcome to The Voodoo Rooms! This handbook covers everything you need to know to work as a bartender during Edinburgh Fringe Festival.

LICENSING & LEGAL
- Scotland operates under the Licensing (Scotland) Act 2005
- All staff must verify ID for customers who appear under 25 (Challenge 25 policy)
- Acceptable ID: Passport, Driving Licence, PASS-accredited proof of age card
- Never serve alcohol to someone who is already visibly intoxicated
- Last orders are called 30 minutes before closing time

DRINK PREPARATION
- Classic cocktails: Espresso Martini (vodka, coffee liqueur, espresso), Negroni (gin, sweet vermouth, Campari)
- Beer: We serve BrewDog Punk IPA, Tennent's Lager, and Innis & Gunn on tap
- Wine list: House red (Malbec), House white (Sauvignon Blanc), Prosecco by the glass
- Always use correct measures: 25ml for spirits, 50ml for wine
- Garnish standards: citrus twist for G&T, olive for Martini, mint for Mojito

CUSTOMER SERVICE
- Greet every customer with a smile within 30 seconds of approach
- During Fringe, expect 300-400 customers per evening shift
- Memorise the top 10 cocktail recipes to serve without referencing notes
- Upsell premium spirits when appropriate (e.g. suggest Monkey Shoulder instead of house Scotch)
- Handle complaints calmly, escalate to manager if necessary

HEALTH & SAFETY
- Fire exits are located at the front entrance and rear kitchen door
- In case of fire: alert customers calmly, do not use lifts, meet at assembly point on West Register Street
- All spills must be cleaned immediately to prevent slip hazards
- Keep the bar area clear of trip hazards at all times
- First aid kit is located behind the bar, near the ice machine

RUSH HOUR PROCEDURES
- Peak times: 10pm-12am during Fringe
- Two staff must always be present at the bar during peak hours
- Use the ticket system for large group orders (6+ people)
- Communicate with floor staff using the radio system
- Last orders drinks must be cleared by 30 minutes after close

USHER ROLE GUIDE
================

RESPONSIBILITIES
- Welcome audiences to shows and check tickets
- Direct guests to correct seating areas
- Assist with accessibility requirements
- Monitor crowd behaviour and ensure fire exits remain clear

TICKET VERIFICATION
- Use the handheld scanner for digital tickets
- Paper tickets: check QR code and date
- If ticket is invalid, direct guest to Box Office on the ground floor
- VIP wristbands: gold for backstage access, green for premium seating

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
- Know the location of all 4 fire exits in the main venue
- Maximum capacity is 250 for the main room, 80 for the upper room
- In emergency: remain calm, use the phrase "Ladies and gentlemen, please make your way calmly to the nearest exit"
- Never leave your post during an emergency until relieved by a manager

TICKET DESK SPECIALIST GUIDE
=============================

RESPONSIBILITIES
- Process ticket sales for walk-in customers
- Handle exchanges and refunds according to policy
- Manage group bookings (10+ people require 24hr advance notice)
- Operate the POS system (Spektrix software)

REFUND POLICY
- Full refund: 48+ hours before show
- 50% refund: 24-48 hours before show
- No refund: less than 24 hours before show
- All refunds require manager approval for amounts over £50

ACCESSIBILITY
- Wheelchair spaces are pre-reserved at the front
- Hearing loop is available in the main room - inform guests at purchase
- Assistance dogs are welcome in all areas
- Large print programmes available on request
